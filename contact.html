Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about traveling to Ghana

Do I need a visa to visit Ghana?

Most visitors need a visa to enter Ghana. You can apply online or at Ghanaian embassies. Some nationalities may be eligible for visa on arrival. We recommend checking with the Ghana Immigration Service for the most current requirements.

What's the best time to visit Ghana?

The best time to visit Ghana is during the dry season (November to March) when the weather is pleasant and there's less rainfall. However, Ghana is a year-round destination, and each season has its unique advantages.

What vaccinations do I need?

Yellow fever vaccination is required for entry into Ghana. We also recommend vaccinations for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and malaria prophylaxis. Please consult with a travel medicine specialist before your trip.

What currency is used in Ghana?

The currency is the Ghanaian Cedi (GHS). US dollars are widely accepted in tourist areas, but it's best to have local currency for smaller purchases. ATMs are available in major cities and towns.

Is Ghana safe for tourists?

Ghana is generally considered one of the safest countries in West Africa for tourists. As with any travel, it's important to stay aware of your surroundings and follow basic safety precautions.

How do I get around Ghana?

Transportation options include domestic flights, buses, taxis, and rental cars. For our tour packages, we provide comfortable, air-conditioned vehicles with experienced drivers for all transportation needs.
